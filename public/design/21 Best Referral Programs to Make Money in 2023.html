21 Best Referral Programs to Make Money

  • ECOMMERCE

Online referral programs produce win-win outcomes for companies and loyal customers alike. Businesses gain new customers while a referring customer earns cash, credits, and discounts for a successful referral. However, not all referral programs are created equally or offer desirable rewards.

In this post, we’ll explain the difference between referral marketing and affiliate marketing and explore some of the best referral programs in the market.

                          What is referral marketing?

                          Referral marketing is a method of promoting products and services through existing customers and users. Companies provide unique links to customers to send to others. Referral programs typically offer discounts and account credits as referral incentives. For instance, Joe sends his referral link to Suzy to save $10 on her first purchase. Referral programs go by different names, including:

                          • Advocate program
                          • Customer reward program
                          • Invite-a-friend
                          • Loyalty program
                          • Refer-a-friend (RAF)
                          • Rewards program

                          Referral marketing vs. affiliate marketing

                          Referral and affiliate marketing do overlap under the umbrella of performance marketing, but they’re different. Affiliate marketing is the promotion of products and services online by affiliate marketers, who are usually full-time content creators, bloggers, and YouTubers. Companies pay affiliates to generate leads and sales. For example, the email marketing company GetResponse has two affiliate programs that pay recurring and bounty commissions for new sales.

                          Given the differences between referral and affiliate marketing, which one should you choose?

                          Promote referral offers if you want to occasionally share deals with your friends and people you know but don’t want to focus on creating content. Go with affiliate marketing if you want to create content, part- or full-time, to passively make extra money.

                          How to choose referral programs

                          Beyond our list, we recommend referring reputable and established companies with rewards and offers that you want to earn. Moreover, focus on recommending products and services that you genuinely love to get others excited.

                          How to promote your referral link

                          It’s easy to promote referral offers to your friends and network. The most common ways are emails, a website, social media, and chat apps.

                          Emails

                          Depending on how often you plan to send emails containing your referral code or link, you may use a free email service (Google, Yahoo, Outlook, etc.) or an email marketing service such as GetResponse. Your email needs partially depend on your intended audience, for example, people you know versus strangers. For the latter, email marketing and spam laws focus on user/recipient rights, including consent, privacy, and data.

                          For instance, suppose you’ll send monthly emails to a small group of high school friends – a free email service would suffice. By contrast, suppose you own a public Facebook group and get members to sign up for your monthly newsletter. In that case, you should consider using an email marketing service. You can start with GetResponse completely free.

                          Website

                          While plenty of free third-party websites and blog platforms exist (Medium, Blogger, etc.), there’s nothing like having a website that you fully control. GetResponse’s free website builder allows you to develop an online presence quickly. It uses artificial intelligence (AI) to create eye-catching websites in a very short time. You can also build a website from scratch with stylish templates and a visual drag-and-drop editor. Best of all, you don’t need to know how to code, making for a hassle-free experience.

                          With a website, you can create a page about your favorite companies and add your referral code or link. Whether on social media or through emails, you can direct your friends to that page. Add images and calls to action to your page for extra pop and more clicks.

                          Start building with GetResponse’s free website builder.

                          Social Media

                          Grab your referral link and post it on Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn. Facebook offers many opportunities, including your profile, pages, and groups. Also, add some text for what your post is about to entice people to click on your link. A link by itself won’t be very appealing and may even appear spammy.

                          If you occasionally upload videos to YouTube, add your link to your descriptions. Consider where you can post your link on Instagram, Pinterest, and Tik Tok.

                          Chat Apps

                          Send your link to your contacts through popular chat and instant messaging apps, including WhatsApp, Messenger, iMessage, Viber, and Signal.
